# Writer pack

Templates for writers: essayists, bloggers, authors, content and technical writers. Covers the decisions that shape a piece and a body of work, plus a pattern template and session log. The published draft carries what you said. The vault carries why you said it that way, what you cut, and what you chose not to claim.

## Templates

| File | Use when |
|---|---|
| `decision.md` | Generic starting point for any writing decision |
| `decision-editorial.md` | The angle, take, framing, or what to cut |
| `decision-project.md` | A piece's format, scope, venue, and where it stands |
| `decision-source.md` | Tracking a claim, figure, source, or permission |
| `pattern.md` | A reusable writing move worth naming |
| `session.md` | What happened in a writing session |

## Decision categories

**Editorial decisions** (`decision-editorial.md`) are the heart of a writer's vault. The angle you chose, the framing you rejected, the point you cut on purpose. None of it survives in the published piece, which shows the version you ran and buries the three you didn't. That is exactly the context that keeps you, and an AI helping you, from re-litigating a call you already made or re-pitching an angle you already killed.

**Project decisions** (`decision-project.md`) track what a piece is and where it's going: format, length, venue, the companion pieces it pairs with, and what has to happen before it ships. Stage lives here (idea, draft, ready, published).

**Source decisions** (`decision-source.md`) are where credibility is won or lost. A figure, a quote, a claim: where it came from, whether it's verified, and any permission or confidentiality constraint (needs attribution, must stay unnamed, get the subject's nod first). Worth a note for anything load-bearing.

## Naming conventions

Decisions: `YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md`, date-prefixed, kebab-case slug derived from the title.

Patterns: `slug.md`, no date prefix. Patterns are timeless; decisions are time-stamped.

Sessions: `YYYY-MM-DD.md`, one file per day, multiple session blocks per file if you write in more than one sitting.
